Welcome to Batanes!

Batanes is the northernmost province of the Philippines, famous for its scenic hills, stone houses, and peaceful atmosphere. It’s known as the “Home of the Winds” and loved for the honesty and kindness of its people, the Ivatans.

Attractions in Batanes

Discover the breathtaking spots that make Batanes a true paradise.

Basco Lighthouse

Basco Lighthouse is located on Naidi Hills in Basco, Batanes, overlooking the vast sea and green landscapes.

Basco Lighthouse

Location: Naidi Hills, Basco, Batanes, Philippines

Description: The Basco Lighthouse is ideal for watching breathtaking sunsets, with panoramic views of the surrounding sea, rolling hills, and traditional Ivatan villages. Its serene atmosphere and cool breezes make it a favorite spot for photography and quiet reflection.

Racuh a Payaman

Racuh a Payaman, also called Marlboro Hills, is a vast grassland known for its scenic beauty and natural rolling hills.

Racuh a Payaman

Location: Sabtang Island, Batanes, Philippines

Description: Racuh a Payaman features wide, green rolling hills that stretch toward the horizon, framed by the blue sea and dramatic cliffs. Its peaceful atmosphere, cool breeze, and open spaces make it perfect for sightseeing, photography, and simply enjoying nature at its most tranquil.

Morong Beach & Arch

Morong Beach is a pristine white sand beach on Sabtang Island, known for its clear waters and calm waves.

Morong Beach and Nakabuang Arch

Location: Sabtang Island, Batanes, Philippines

Description: Morong Beach offers a peaceful escape with soft sand and turquoise waters ideal for relaxation and photography. The Nakabuang Arch, rising dramatically from the sea, provides a stunning natural frame for the ocean and sky, making it one of Batanes’ most photographed spots.

Delicacies in Batanes

Discover the unique and traditional flavors of Ivatan cuisine that reflect the culture and simplicity of island life.

Uvud / Uved

Made from finely grated banana pith mixed with fish or meat, Uvud is a hearty Ivatan comfort food usually served with broth.

Supas

During fiestas or festival, Supas or balencyana is rice cooked in turmeric pulp that’s often mixed with garlic and pork.

Vunes

Vunes is a traditional dish made from dried gabi stalks, cooked with pork and coconut milk — similar to Bicol’s laing.

Interesting Facts About Batanes

Discover amazing facts that show the beauty, honesty, and culture of the Ivatans.

1
The Northernmost and Smallest Province

Batanes is the northernmost and smallest province in the Philippines (in both size and population). It is closer to Taiwan than it is to the Philippine mainland of Luzon.

2
The Stone House (Bahay na Bato) and The Vakul

It is known for its traditional stone houses made of lime and rock, which have thick cogon grass roofs. This design is built to withstand strong typhoons. The women wear a distinct headgear called a Vakul, which is made from palm fiber for protection against rain and heat.

3
Zero Crime Rate

Batanes is famous for its near zero-crime rate. The people are known for their honesty, which can be seen in the famous Honesty Coffee Shop in Ivana, where customers pay and make change themselves without any staff present.
