Top Attractions in Batanes

Learn more about the most beautiful destinations across the islands.

Basco Lighthouse

Location: Naidi Hills, Basco, Batanes, Philippines

Short History: Built in 2003, the Basco Lighthouse serves both as a navigational aid for ships and as a popular tourist spot. It stands as a symbol of safety and resilience, overlooking the dramatic coastlines of Batanes.

How to Get There: From Basco town proper, you can take a tricycle for about 10 minutes to reach the lighthouse.

Description: The Basco Lighthouse is ideal for watching breathtaking sunsets, with panoramic views of the surrounding sea, rolling hills, and traditional Ivatan villages. Its serene atmosphere and cool breezes make it a favorite spot for photography and quiet reflection.

House of Dakay

Location: Ivana, Batanes, Philippines

Short History: The House of Dakay is the oldest surviving stone house in Batanes, built in the 1880s by the Ivatan people. It was constructed to withstand strong typhoons and earthquakes, showcasing the resilience and ingenuity of traditional Ivatan architecture.

How to Get There: From Basco town proper, you can take a tricycle or van heading to Ivana, which takes around 30–40 minutes.

Description: This historic house features thick limestone walls, cogon grass roofing, and a design that reflects the Ivatan way of life. Visitors can explore the structure and learn about the culture and traditions of the Ivatans while appreciating the house’s durability and timeless beauty.

Mount Carmel Chapel

Location: Tukon, Basco, Batanes, Philippines

Short History: Mount Carmel Chapel, commonly known as Tukon Chapel, was built to serve the spiritual needs of the local community. Its stone structure reflects the traditional Ivatan architectural style, blending harmoniously with the natural hilltop surroundings.

How to Get There: From Basco town proper, you can reach the chapel by tricycle or private vehicle in about 15–20 minutes, traveling uphill to Tukon.

Description: Perched on a hill, the chapel offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains, rolling hills, and distant sea. Its serene and peaceful environment makes it ideal for prayer, reflection, and photography, especially during sunrise or sunset.

Racuh a Payaman

Location: Sabtang Island, Batanes, Philippines

Short History: Racuh a Payaman, also called Marlboro Hills, is a vast grassland known for its scenic beauty and natural rolling hills. It has long been a grazing ground for local livestock and a favorite spot for locals and tourists to enjoy Batanes’ serene landscapes.

How to Get There: From Sabtang port, take a tricycle or van toward the hills. The drive offers scenic views of coastal cliffs, farmland, and traditional Ivatan houses.

Description: Racuh a Payaman features wide, green rolling hills that stretch toward the horizon, framed by the blue sea and dramatic cliffs. Its peaceful atmosphere, cool breeze, and open spaces make it perfect for sightseeing, photography, and simply enjoying nature at its most tranquil.

Vayang Rolling Hills

Location: Basco, Batanes, Philippines

Short History: Vayang Rolling Hills is a famous scenic area in Basco known for its endless green slopes and panoramic views. It has been a grazing area for local cattle and a favorite spot for both locals and tourists to enjoy the natural beauty of Batanes.

How to Get There: From Basco town proper, you can take a tricycle or private vehicle to the hills. The drive takes around 10–15 minutes and offers beautiful views of the coastline and surrounding landscapes.

Description: Vayang Rolling Hills features gently sloping green hills that stretch toward the horizon, offering a peaceful and breezy atmosphere. Visitors can enjoy hiking, photography, and panoramic views of the sea, mountains, and Basco town below, especially during sunrise or sunset.

Morong Beach and Nakabuang Arch

Location: Sabtang Island, Batanes, Philippines

Short History: Morong Beach is a pristine white sand beach on Sabtang Island, known for its clear waters and calm waves. Nearby, the Nakabuang Arch is a natural rock formation shaped by centuries of wind and sea erosion, becoming an iconic landmark of the area.

How to Get There: From Sabtang port, take a tricycle or local transport to Morong Beach. The journey takes around 20–30 minutes, passing by scenic coastal views and traditional Ivatan villages.

Description: Morong Beach offers a peaceful escape with soft sand and turquoise waters ideal for relaxation and photography. The Nakabuang Arch, rising dramatically from the sea, provides a stunning natural frame for the ocean and sky, making it one of Batanes’ most photographed spots.
